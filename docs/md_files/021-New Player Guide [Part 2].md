# New Player Guide [Part 2]

## Project Genesis | Mission 1 | Maintenance Duty [Part 1]

### Mission 1 Pre-Start Items

Part 2 of this guide covers "Project Genesis", a new plotline.  That name may sound
familiar and that's because it's the other Game Start for the Cradle of Humanity DLC.
Picking a Game Start in X4 does not lock you into the plotline for that Game Start.  In
fact, you can experience all the plotlines offered by all the Game Starts no matter which
Game Start you choose.

Before you take off from the dock, do the following:

* Repair your ship if needed
* Upgrade the engine to a Travel Engine Mk2
  + You lose out on boost stats, but the travel drive spool up time to get to maximum
    speed is greatly reduced and the travel speed is significantly faster.  Both of
    these stats will be useful for this mission.
* Purchase 2 Apprentice Service crew members
  + We'll use them for later

This mission is focused on exploration and discovering a path to the objective.  The
final destination is in a sector that is not shown on your map.  Here are some tips to
help you discover the route to the final destination:

### Using the Mission Ticker to Navigate to the Destination

The Mission Ticker in the lower-left shows two important lines regarding your
destination:

* Objective Line (>)
  + The top ">" line always shows the objective
  + For this mission, it's "> Fly to: Oberth"
* Recommended Next Action Line (>>)
  + The bottom ">>" line is the recommended next action for the objective
  + For this mission, a couple of recommended actions will appear:
  + ">> Fly to: Asteroid Belt"
    - After completing the recommended action, it will update to the next recommended
      one. An example is after reaching Asteroid Belt, it will change to
      ">> Fly to: Jupiter" for this mission.
  + OR
  + ">> Find way to: Saturn 1"
    - If the next recommended sector is not in your map, then the recommended action
      will change from "Fly to:" to "Find way to:"
    - The gate for the next recommended sector will always be in the sector you're
      currently in, but it's up to you to find it
    - There may be multiple gates to choose from also, so you will have to fly through
      each one and check

### Discovering the Path to the Oberon

Auto-pilot can be used to automatically fly to the last known sector en route to your
destination.  For this mission, if you haven't done any additional exploration, then the
auto-pilot will take you into Jupiter and then disengage because the gate for the next
sector is not known.

#### Visually Finding Gates

Now, the gate you need to go through next is in Jupiter, but you'll need to find where
it is.  The easiest way to find a gate is to visually check around for an object that
looks like a gate and then fly towards it.  If only there was something that could help
us zoom in and see objects far away.

Introducing Visual Enhancement Goggles, or VE Goggles for short.  Press y to zoom in
and shift+y to zoom out.  If the zoom isn't working, make sure you're in Cockpit view
and not External view.

From here, rotate the ship around, zoom in with your googles, and see if you can spot an
object that looks like a gate.  If you can't spot one, it may be too far aware so you'll
need to explore the sector some more, zoom, and see if you can spot it.  Gates can be
relatively close to each other, or spread far apart, it varies from sector-to-sector.

Using the Long Range Scanner is an option as well, but it is limited to a 200km range
which is actually a shorter distance than using the VE Goggles to visually spot gates.

Repeat your chosen process until you reach the destination sector.  The X4 universe is
huge so expect to repeat this process often until you get all the sectors and their
connections mapped out.  That's part of the fun of exploration!

### Deliver AGI Processor for Dr. Rick Feynman

After you get close enough to the Oberon, you'll receive a fetch quest to get an AGI
Processor for Dr. Rick Feynman.  There are a few items to point out after you get this
task:

* Check your Mission Manager (Shift+M) and you'll notice the mission has updated with
  the new information and also a new Objective
* Open the map and look at the station Dr. Rick Fenman points you to.  You'll notice
  there is a hat with sunglasses and a yellow exclamation mark next to it.
  + The hat withsunglasses means there is a mission-related person to speak with
    - If you left-click on the station, it'll show the name of the mission-related
      person; in this case, it's Pat Mason
  + The yellow exclamation mark means the station has a mission objective
    - Even if you mark the mission inactive, the icon will still be there but with
      in a faded yellow color
    - If you right-click on the station, there will be an option to "Show mission
      (Maintenance) Duty" which will bring up the mission details
    - The "Show mission ..." option applies to any object with a yellow exclamation mark
      so you can see what mission it applies to

Be sure to look at the Message Ticker box to see what the current objective is
(Top Line: >) and the recommended next action for it (Bottom Line: >>). And yes, like
stations, ships can also have docks that you can land on.

As usual, when you complete the mission, feel free to explore before starting the new
one.  When you're ready, proceed to the next section.

## Project Genesis | Mission 2 | Maintenance Duty [Part 2]

Do the following tutorial:

* Intermediate Tutorials
  1. Orders
      - Includes setting Behaviour

For this mission, we're going to make use of one of the service members on our ship.
We've been piloting our ship the whole time but wouldn't it be nice to take a break?  If
yes, I've got the solution for you!

Any ship we fly can also be flown by a crew member we hire.  In order for someone to fly
a ship, they have to be promoted to Pilot for that ship.  Here is how we do that:

1. Bring up Current Ship Info using the hotkey (Default: Shift+Enter)
2. In the top button bar under "Information", press the "Crew" button
3. Near the bottom of this menu is a section labeled "Full Crew List" which lists the
   crewmembers currently assigned to the ship
4. Press the "+" next to the crew member to fully reveal all their stats.  We're looking
   for the one with the best "Piloting" rating i.e. the most stars.
5. Check both crew members and see who has the highest rating (we only care about
   Piloting)
6. Right-click on that crew member and select "Promote to Pilot"
    + The other service crew member can help with repairs to your ship when systems
      get damaged.  We'll explore more about crew members later.
7. Congratulations, you now have an NPC pilot for your ship!
8. For the NPC pilot to take over, you have to get your butt off the pilot's seat using
   "Get Up from Chair" hotkey (Default: Ctrl+d)
9. Once you leave your chair, the NPC pilot will show up from God knows where and plant
   their butt on the pilot's seat

As show in the Orders tutorial, you can now select your shop from the map and issue
orders to the pilot. What separates the NPC pilot from using Autopilot is:

* The NPC pilot can dock at ships or stations
* You can order the ship around to do things even if you're not on it

So now you have the option to let the NPC pilot fly your ship around.  The NPC pilot
can't do everything you can so you'll still have to take over, but they're great when
you need to dock at a station far away.

To take over piloting your ship, walk up to the NPC pilot and press the "Interact"
hotkey (Default: f) to "Take Command of Ship."

Try to order the NPC pilot to each of the objectives for this mission.

Complete the mission and when you're ready, proceed to the next section.

## Boso Ta | Mission 1 | Displaced

### Intro

This mission introduces many concepts including ones I've added as part of the guide.
Since a lot is covered, I've made a walkthrough to help learn the new concepts in
manageable chunks.

### Part 1 - Welcome to Grand Exchange I

Just when you thought everything was going smooth, bam, you get transported halfway
across the universe!  Open your map and you'll see you're in "Grand Exchange I" now.
For reference, you were previously in "Gaian Prophecy" (to the east) before getting
transported.

Follow the directions from Boso Ta and float your way to the spaceship he sent over for
you.  

After you arrive to your new ship, you'll be tasked with building a Dock for the
asteroid station that transported with you.  As a bonus, you own an additional ship now
which means you have a fleet of two ships!

### Part 2 - The Player Headquarters (PHQ)

The asteroid station is now called "Grand Exchange 1 Headquarters" on the map.  This
headquarters is also known by another name, the PHQ or "Player Headquarters."  I'll
refer to it as the PHQ for the rest of the guide.

Open the map, right-click on the PHQ, left-click on "Plan Build" item.  The game will go
into a station building tutorial similar to the one followed earlier.

The first step of the tutorial is to choose a dock and **IT IS VERY IMPORTANT** that the
dock you choose is the "1M6S Standard" one.  **DO NOT** choose the Terran ones!

Materials are needed to build the dock module and the other two docks require materials
that are significantly harder to aquire at this point in the game.  Without delving into
it too much, the other two docks require materials that are only created by Terran
production stations and they're on the other side of the universe.  

Continue on with the tutorial.  When you get to the point to assign a Builder, don't
worry if one doesn't show up on the map, we'll go hunting for it.

In the Station Building advanced tutorial we took earlier, it started out with
purchasing a plot and building the station within the plot.  Think of the PHQ as having
a pre-allocated plot and instead of building in open space, everything is built around a
giant asteroid.

### Part 3 - Building a Dock Module for the PHQ

Do the following tutorial:

* Advanced Tutorials
  1. Station Building
      - Remember what the Builder ship icon on the map looks like

Stations need two things to build new modules:

1. Resources aka Wares (I'll use Wares for the rest of this guide)
2. A Builder that uses the wares to build the new module

Optionally, credits may be required if the wares cannot be produced by the station
itself or they cannot be delivered by the player to the station.  Credits will be used
by the station to purchase wares delivered by NPC traders.

In the tutorial, we gave the station credits to purchase the required wares from NPC
traders.  For this guide though, we'll supply the station with the constructions wares
it needs ourselves.  Use the "Property Owned" hotkey (Default: shift+P) and you'll now
see the PHQ listed under "Stations & Subordinates."  To access the station construction
screen, right-click on the PHQ and left-click on the "Plan Build" item.

To figure out what construction wares the station needs, look in the right windows for
the line:

|+| Resources needed to complete changes

Expand that line and you'll see we're only missing 108 Energy Cells to complete the
construction of the dock.  Its time to take matters into our own hands and get those
Energy Cells.

Exit out of the construction screen and bring up the Property Owned menu
(Default: Shift+P). Click the |+| to expand the line for the PHQ and you may notice a
new item listed there.  Specifically, this line in orange:

|+| Dock Modules (0%)      ! 0:40:00

The above line is the progress for the Dock Module and has the following progress
states:

1. When no Builder is assigned and required wares are unfulfilled, the line will be
   ornage with an exclamation mark
2. When a Builder is assigned but required wares are unfulfilled, the line will remain
   orange with the exclamation mark but the timer will reduce from 40 minutes to 8
   minutes
3. If the required wares are fulfilled, but no Builder is assigned, the line will turn
   white and the timer will remain at 40 minutes
4. When the Builder is assigned and the wares are fulfilled, the line will turn white,
   the timer will reduce from 40 minutes to 8 minutes and the countdown will begin

The offer is open for NPC Traders to deliver the Energy Cells and we'll know if they've
been delivered if the progress line changes to a white color.  But we're going to beat
them to get the Energy Cells delivered first, right?  Right...?

### Part 4 - Faction Relations and Who Doesn't Like You

* References:
  + [Egosoft Wiki | Reputation Effects](https://wiki.egosoft.com/X4%20Foundations%20Wiki/Manual%20and%20Guides/Objects%20in%20the%20Game%20Universe/Factions/%28Player%29/Reputation%20Effects/)

Do the following tutorial:

* Intermediate Tutorials
  1. Faction Relations and Licenses

In the X4 universe, there are several races and each race has it's own factions.
Relations are associated with factions, not with the race as a whole.  To view your
current faction relations, use the hotkey to open the Diplomacy Menu (Default: n).

In this menu, we have several items to observe:

1. The color of the faction
    + White: Neutral or Friendly
    + Pink: Enemy
    + Red: Hostile
2. The Relation number/status
    + This number affects how favorably a member of that faction responds to you in
      communications and other activies as well
    + -10 and below: Docking/trading with faction units is denied
    + -20 and below: Faction ships/stations will attack all player combat ships
    + -25 and below: Faction ships/stations will attack all player property
3. The three-letter faction identifie:r
    + When viewing objects on the map, the three-letter identifier is used for factions

Close out of the Diplomacy Menu and open the Map.  On the Map, friendly objects are
in a blue color instead of a white. Enemy and Hostile objects remain the same color of
pink and red.  

There are several ways to improve faction relations and a diplomacy system as well.  We
will cover those topics later as we have enough to do at the moment.

### Part 5 - Using Comms to Ask for Directions

To get Energy Cells, we need to find a "Solar Power Plant" or a "Trading Station."
Trading Stations are much more rare to find so we will focus on finding Solar Power
Plants isntead.

A Solar Power Plant may be near our PHQ already, or it may not, everyone's game is
different when it comes to what stations are in a sector.  For this guide, we will
assume one is not nearby so we can cover how to find them.  Even if one is nearby,
ignore it and let's see if we can find another one with some exploring.

Since we're in an unexplored part of the universe, let's get some assistance in finding
where things are.  In X4, we can open Comms to other ships and ask them for directions
to specific Points of Interests (POIs).

Here is how communication works with another ship (communication cannot be initiated
with stations):

* The ship you communicate with must be in radar range of the ship the player is
  currently located in
  + The player doesn't have to be piloting the ship, just aboard it
  + NPC ships cannot communicate to other ships outside your radar range on your behalf
* The ship must be a blue color
  + You can communicate with pink/red ships as well but they provide any information
* If asking for Directions, even if the ship is blue, there is a chance it will
  decline to give you help based on your relation status
  + If this happens, try another blue ship

Now, let's find a ship to communicate with on the map:

1. Make sure your ship is unselected, otherwise, a different set of actions shows up
   when you right-click on the ship you want to open comms with
2. Select any Teladi Company (TEL) ship in range of your player ship
3. Right-click on it and left-click on Comm
4. Ask "Where can I find...?"
    + If they decline you, try again with another TEL ship
5. Answer with "Way to: Wharf"

If all goes well, they'll send you the information and you'll now have a Guidance
Mission to get to the Wharf.  Check the Mission Ticker and you'll see the following:

Objective Line: > Fly to: Unknown Station
Next Recommended Action Line: >> Fly to: Unknown Sector

Here is the cool part about asking for directions specifically, this logic does not
apply to anything else.  If you use Autopilot, it will automatically take you to the
station, even if it's in an unknown part of space.  Because it only works with
Autopilot, that means you'll have to pilot the ship because NPC's can't use autopilot
(a limitation of NPC pilots).  Again, this only works if you're asking for directions
and then use Autopilot to get there, otherwise it's the manual method of finding gates
yourself like you did previously to find the Oberon.

So take over the ship, put on the Autopilot, and watch the scenery as it takes you to
the directed Wharf in Unknown Space.

### Part 6 - Lookout for Builders and Solar Power Plants

While the Autopilot is navigating there, open the map and watch for the following:

* Solar Power Plants
* Builders
  + On the map, they are identified as a "Construction Vessel" when you hover over their
    icon
  + Their map icon looks like a tear drop/oval with a tail shaped like a diamond-shaped
    heart

Hopefully both will show up on your way there.  The Solar Power Plant we can visit later
but if you see a non-hostile Builder, immediately right-click on it and select:

"Hire for building > Grand Exchange I Headquarters"

It does not matter if you don't have all the wares needed to build the dock module, the
Builder will wait at the station until you do.

### Part 7 - Deploying a Sattelite to Maintain Trade Information for Wharf

Eventually the Autopilot will take you to the sector "Windfall I Union Summit" and park
you near the "VIG Vigor Syndicate Wharf."  Along the way, we discovered a couple of new
sectors.  After you arrive, open the Missions Menu (Default: shift+M), select the
"Guidance Mission" button and abort the mission.  The Guidance Mission and it's yellow
line will remain until we cancel it or replace it with a new Guidance Mission.

While we're here, let's buy a couple of things from this shipyard and let's our NPC
Pilot do the driving.  When an NPC Pilot is in control, we can actually do the
purchase from the map and the NPC Pilot will automatically take care of docking at the
station to get the items.  This applies to anything we want to buy for ship upgrades or
equipment.

From the Map, do the following:

1. Select the Player Ship (Kestral Sentinel)
2. Right-click on the Wharf
3. Select "Upgrade / Repair at..."
4. Purchase the following items under Consumables:
    + Buy 1 Satellite
    + Buy 2 more Flares to max out at 4/4
      - Always max out your flares when you can

After you confirm the order, the NPC Pilot will automatically dock at the Wharf and
the items will be transferred to the ship.  

Since this is a Wharf / Equipment Dock we can buy things from, let's keep track of it
for future purposes.  After a station leaves radar range of ship, trade information is
still available for 5 realtime hours.  5 hours can pass by fast in this game so to
ensure we can buy from this Wharf at any time, let's drop a satellite near it.  

Since our NPC Pilot is piloting the ship, here are the steps to deploy the Satellite:

1. Open the map and select your Kestral ship
2. Right-click on an empty space near the Wharf
3. Under "Deploy at Position"
    + Select Civilian > Satellite
4. Make sure the Wharf is within the sphere of the Satellite.  If it's not, you can
   click and drag the ship icon in the center of the sphere to move the deployment of
   the Satellite.

Excellent, now we can buy from this Wharf at any time without worrying about the trade
information timer expiring on it.

Now, let's take a roadtrip on the Highway which we've only see in the "Highways and
Gates" Basic tutorial.  When you're ready, proceed to the next section.

### Part 6 - Roadtrip to the Core Highway Network

The Core Highway isn't an option when asking for directions so we'll have to find it
manually.  The good news is the Highway is in an adjacent sector to the sector we're in,
"Windfall I Union Summit."  The other good news is the gate is within VE Goggles zoom
range from the Wharf.  Explore the other gates to see what sector they connect to.

Once you reach the sector "Silent Witness I," you can access the Highway.  Open the
map and you'll see two thin blue lines in the middle.  That is our Core Highway Network,
that is where our future lies.  

Haul your butt over to the Highway and converge on to one of the lanes (doesn't matter
which one).  From here, enjoy the ride as the Highway takes you on a tour of unexplored
sectors.

Now, while we're cruising on the Highway, let's check on our Kukri that got left back
in Terran Space when we got teleported with the Asteroid Station.

### Part 8 - Using the Kukri NPC Pilot to Explore

Luckily (or was it planned...), we got an NPC Pilot for our Kukri so we can still
control it even halfway across the Universe.  While we're cruising on the Highway, let's
give our Kukri something to do.

Right now, there is no known path from where the Kukri is, "Gaian Prophecy", to where
our PHQ is in "Grand Exchange I" but we can fix that.  Like we've done before, it's a
matter of finding the jump gates and making incremental progress in the direction of our
destination (in this case, moving East).  

Since the Kukri has an NPC Pilot, our options for finding gates are different.  We've
got three methods we can try:

1. Issue the Explore command to the NPC Pilot
    + I generally don't recommend this command for exploration because it is very slow
    + Exploration aside, the Explore command is the only command that can be used on
      an NPC Pilot to order them through a gate to an Unknown Sector
2. Issue multiple "Fly and Wait" to explore the sector
    + Requires the most micro-management but can ensure you get full coverage of the
      sector (including the stations)
3. Follow NPC Trader ships and let them lead you to a gate
    + This is the method I recommend and we'll use for the Kukri

### Part 9 - Following NPC Traders to Find Gates in an Unexplored Sector

Here are steps you can follow to use NPC Trader ships to find Gates:

* Special Note about Issuing Orders for NPC Pilots
  + When assigning orders to NPC Pilots, the newest order should overwrite the old but
    that doesn't always happen.  If you encounter a situation where an NPC Pilot is
    ignoring your new orders, it's most likely because the new orders are getting
    queue'd behind an existing order
  + If the NPC Pilot is not responding to new orders, try removing all orders and then
    issue the new order again
    - To remove all orders, right-click on the NPC ship and under the "Orders" section,
      select "Remove all orders" or "Remove all orders and assignments"
    - Assignments refers to the "default behavior" and the above command resets the
      default behavior to "Hold Position"

1. Fly the Kukri to the center of the sector
2. Once the center is reached, check for Trader ships (ships with Trader, Freighter,
   Delivery, etc... in their name) that are heading East.  
    + If no ships are here, keep moving East until you find a Trader moving East also
    + Focus on Traders for this method, avoid tailing other ships such as Builders,
      Miners or Scavanegers
3. When a Trader moving East is spotted, with the Kukri select, right-click on the
   Trader ship and select "Follow until further notice"
4. If we're lucky, the ship will take us to the gate to the next sector but the NPC
   Pilot will not follow them through a gate to an Unknown Sector
    + If we're unlucky, the ship actually takes us to a gate away from our destination
    + It's also possible that the ship we're following is faster than the Kukri and
      went through the gate before it showed up in range of the Kukri's radar.  If that
      happened, just find another ship to following going in the same general direction.
    + Using this method takes some experience in figuring out if a ship is going to
      lead us through a gate but once you get the hang of it, finding gates using NPC
      Pilots become significantly easier.
5. Give the Kukri the order to Explore the gate
    + If the Kukri is now following the order, check the Special Note above about
      issuing orders to NPC Pilots
    + Explore is used because Fly and Wait can't be used to traverse gates to Unknown
      Sectors
6. Repeat the process in the next sector
    + There won't always be a gate to take us East in the Universe.  Sometimes we'll
      have to go North, or South, or even West and then move East from there.  Watching
      the Trader flow of traffic helps us determine which positions the gates are in the
      sector.

Getting the Kukri to "Grand Exchange I" is purely optional and more of something to do
when we have downtime with the Kestral (such as when its traversing the Highway).
Multitasking during travel downtime is a common task in X4, especially as the empire
grows.

Careful also about leaving the Kukri out in the open.  Once it leaves Terran space, it
is much vulnerable to attack by hostile factions.  An often occurence in X4 is you'll
get distracted by something and when you come back to check on your ship, it's been
destroyed.  Having the Kukri dock at a station while you're doing other things will keep
it protected.  Also, always be saving!

### Part 10 - Finding Energy Cells Sell Offers

Do the following tutorials:

* Intermediate Tutorials
  1. Trade Orders
      - Includes Trade Filter

Checking back on the Kestral, it should of completed several trips on the Highway by
now.  During those trips, it will have mapped out all the stations picked up by it's
radar.  Between navigating to the Wharf, then to the Highway, then taking a round-trip
on the Highway, hopefully a Solar Power Plant popped up somewhere on the map.  

To find a Solar Power Plant, we could manually browse through the map, or we could use
the Trade Filter to show us which stations are selling Energy Cells.  If we did find a
Solar Power Plant or a Trading Station that sells Energy Cells, it'll show up on the
Trade Filter.

To find a station selling Energy Cells, do the following:

1. Open the map
2. Enable the Trade Filter if it's not already enabled (Default: 1)
3. Search for "Energy Cells"
4. With the map centered on the PHQ, zoom out until you see a sell offer for
   Energy Cells.  The more you zoom out, the more offers you may see but we want the
   one that is closest to the PHQ
5. Close out of the map once you've located the station you want to buy Energy Cells
   from

If you no sell offers for Energy Cells popped up, proceed to the next section, otherwise
you can optionally skip it and go to the section after.

### Part 11 - Following NPC Energy Traders to Find Solar Power Plant Stations

In the very rare case no sell offers for Energy Cells can be found, we do have another
familiar method we can employ to find one.  Similar to how we followed traders to find
where a gate is in a sector, we can follow "Energy Traders" to find where a Solar
Power Plant is.

In X4, an NPC trader's name correspond to the type of wares they're trading in.  For
traders dealing with Energy Cells, they'll have either "Energy Trader" or
"Energy Freighter" in their name.  Following one will lead you to either a station
it's delivering Energy Cells to, or a Solar Power Plant that it's going to buy Energy
Cells from.

Similar to following NPC Traders for the Kukri, if the trader goes into an unexplored
sector, you will have to manually issue the Explore order to traverse the gate and
then issue the follow order again to keep following it.

Once a Solar Power Plant has been found, proceed to the next section.

### Part 12 - Purchasing and Delivering Energy Cells to Complete the Dock Module

Do the following tutorials:

* Intermediate Tutorials
  1. Trading

Open the Property Owned menu (Default: Shift+P) and see if construction has started, or
maybe even finished for the Dock Module.  It's possible NPC Traders delivered the
needed Energy Cells while we were exploring.

If Energy Cells are still needed, then buy them from the nearest Solar Power Plant.

Delivering the Energy Cells to the PHQ is a different than a normal trade.
Specifically, all stations have something called a "Build Storage."  Like the name
implies, the Build Storage is specifically used to store wares for construction.  A
common mistake for new players is to deliver the wares to the station instead of the
Build Storage.

After the Energy Cells have been purchased, here are the steps to deliver them to the
PHQ's Build Storage:

1. Select the Kestral
2. Right-click on the PHQ
3. Select "Transfer wares with Build Storage"
    + The difference between Trade and Transfer is the menu presented for transferring
      the wares.  The Transfer menu will show all wares currently needed for module
      construction and the amounts needed for each ware.
4. After the transfer is complete (and assuming a Builder has been assigned and has
   arrived at the PHQ), the construction shall commence!

While the Dock module is constructing, try exploring some more with the Kukri.

### Part 13 - Dock Module Completed

When the Dock Module is completed, we'll be contacted by Boso Ta to land on the newly
created dock for our PHQ.  Land the Kestral on the dock and look around, you've just
landed your ship on your first station.

Follow Boso Ta's instructions which will lead to the next mission.  When ready, proceed
to the next section.  Finally, woohoo!

## Boso Ta | Mission 2 | Escape Plan

In addition to specific POIs, asking for directions can also be used to create a

    screen
    - This function only applies when you're given a mission and it has an unknown
      sector in the route.  We could of used this method when navigating to the
      Oberth, but I wanted you to do it the hard way first :p.
    - You will have to use the Hard way if a mission isn't given for your destination
  + Bottom Line changes from >> Find a way to: Napiloeos to >> Fly to: Napileos
  + Once we got the directions, we can use Autopilot to get there or direct our NPC
    Pilot to Explore the gate
