## How to be a Jetpack professional in X4

### Credit: AkevaBanshee

### Intro

1. Go into settings and rebind the "Stop engines" keybind in Controls>General Controls Settings. This will be important later.
2. Drop a quicksave in case you get too far from the airlock or can't find it you can reload the save and try again.
3. Now your outside take a quick moment to orient your self & consider dropping a "start guidance to target" marker on your ship so you know where its at and how far away. Optional since you dropped a quicksave.
4. Now your outside get used to the controls. The spacesuit will tend to drift around. The circular radicle indicates your direction of travel, but it can be offscreen. If this happens hit the boost button to get it back in front of you. But be careful not to be facing a wall or a ship. If you are that's where the Keybind "Stop engines" come in.
5. Returning to the airlock and docking. All you need to do is get close to the ship and either right click it bringing up the context menu or (default keybind) Shift+D and a green docking hologram & docking lights will appear.

### Additional Notes

- Make liberal use of the Boost and Stop Engines keybinds to enhance your maneuverability and navigate tight corners.
- Stop Engines is great for stopping drift and preparing for quick changes in directions.
- Boost is good for quickly changing direction and minimizing drift.
- Conder using alternate flight mode/steering mode (default keybind) Shift+Spacebar to toggle to the smooth steering. Use what one feel most comfortable.
- I would recommend going into settings and turning off "Auto-Roll" This can cause vertigo in some players and in space Up-Down is what you decide it is, or how your brain interprets it. At least try this setting to see if it works for you. It's found under Settings>Game Settings>Auto-roll
- Suit has more than 30 IRL minutes of power. Don't worry about it too much and if you feel anxiety about it remember it's a game and you dropped a quicksave.
